The Foundations

Disney's 12 Principles of Animation serve as the definitive guide for creating realistic movement. From Squash and Stretch to Timing, these foundations allow characters to feel alive and believable.

01

Squash & Stretch

The most vital principle. Defines weight and volume by distorting shape during movement.

02

Anticipation

Preparing the viewer for an action. Like a spring coiling before release.

03

Staging

Directing the audience's eye. Every pose must tell a clear, distraction-free story.

04

Pose to Pose

Drawing key frames first to ensure the character hits the right marks.

05

Follow Through

When the body stops, extremities continue moving to simulate inertia.

06

Slow In & Out

Adding frames at the start and end of movement for realistic acceleration.

07

Arcs

Natural movements follow a curved path. Arcs make motion feel organic.

08

Secondary Action

Supporting actions that add character depth and personality.

09

Timing

The physics of time. Correct timing gives objects their specific weight.

10

Exaggeration

Pushing reality to the extreme to make actions more dynamic.

11

Solid Drawing

Giving drawings a 3D feel through volume, weight, and balance.

12

Appeal

The charisma of the character that connects with the audience.
